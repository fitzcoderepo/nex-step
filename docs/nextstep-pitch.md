# NexStep — The Pitch

## The Problem

Every customer-facing business deals with the same drain on their support team — repetitive, low-complexity requests that have known answers but still require a human to respond to individually at scale. Questions like:

- "I can't log in"
- "My order hasn't arrived"
- "How do I reset my password"

These requests are predictable, structured, and solvable — yet they consume a disproportionate amount of support team time and resources.

## Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

**Static FAQ pages** put the burden on the user to find the right answer themselves, and don't account for the fact that the same symptom can have multiple causes.

**Knowledge bases** are better, but still require users to search, read, and self-diagnose without any guidance.

**AI chatbots** introduce unpredictability. Businesses in regulated industries or with precise support workflows can't always afford an answer that's 90% right. There's hallucination risk, unexpected responses, and ongoing prompt engineering to maintain.

**Hiring more support staff** is expensive and doesn't scale cleanly with growth.

## Where NexStep Fits

NexStep sits in a compelling middle ground — more intelligent and guided than a FAQ or knowledge base, but more predictable and controllable than an AI chatbot.

Businesses author exactly what users see and are guided toward. Every branch, every response, every resolution is intentional and approved. No hallucinations, no unexpected answers, no black box.

## The Secondary Problem NexStep Solves

Most businesses that try to build something like this end up hard-coding it into their own codebase — making it brittle, expensive to update, and dependent on developer involvement every time something changes.

NexStep gives non-technical staff the tools to own and maintain their support content themselves, through an intuitive visual editor, without ever touching the codebase.

## The End-User Perspective

A business won't see the support ticket reduction they're paying for if their customers find the experience frustrating. End users don't want to write a ticket and wait. They don't want to dig through a knowledge base. They want their problem solved immediately, without talking to anyone, and without having to articulate their issue perfectly.

A well-built decision tree delivers exactly that — an immediate, structured path to resolution that feels fast and frictionless. But decision trees have a reputation for being frustrating when done poorly. The kind where none of the options match your situation, or you hit a dead end that just tells you to call support anyway.

That experience erodes customer trust and defeats the purpose entirely.

## The Core Product Principle

**For NexStep to sell to businesses, it has to work for their customers.**

This is the philosophy NexStep is built around. The businesses are NexStep's customers, but their customers are NexStep's users. If the end-user experience fails, businesses don't get the results they paid for — and they churn.

This means NexStep isn't just a tool for building decision trees easily. It's a tool designed to help businesses build decision trees that *actually work* — with guardrails, best practice guidance, and feedback mechanisms baked into the authoring experience itself.

## The Pitch

> **Reduce support ticket volume, empower non-technical teams to own their support content, and do it with something more reliable and controllable than AI.**

NexStep gives businesses a guided, decision-tree-based support experience that leads customers to resolution — step by step, branch by branch — without the unpredictability of AI or the limitations of static documentation. And because NexStep is built around the end-user experience as much as the authoring experience, businesses don't just get a tool. They get results.
